Geography

Lakshadweep is India’s smallest Union Territory, located in the Arabian Sea. The name Lakshadweep means "a hundred thousand islands" in Sanskrit, although the territory consists of just 36 small islands and atolls.

It lies approximately 200 to 440 km off the southwestern coast of India and is known for its pristine coral atolls, crystal-clear lagoons, white sandy beaches, and vibrant marine biodiversity.

Major Attractions

  • Kavaratti Island: Administrative capital and home to beautiful mosques and beaches.
  • Agatti Island: Has the only airport in the territory and known for water sports and coral reefs.
  • Minicoy Island: Known for its Maldivian culture and lighthouse built in 1885.
  • Bangaram Atoll: A stunning uninhabited island ideal for diving and snorkeling.
  • Kalpeni Island: Offers picturesque lagoons and kayaking opportunities.
