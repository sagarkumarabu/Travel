Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach, also known as Papanasham Beach, is situated in Varkala Town of Varkala Municipality in Thiruvananthapuram district, Kerala, India. The beach borders the Arabian Sea, part of the Indian Ocean.

Sights

Varkala Beach is the only place in southern Kerala where cliffs are found adjacent to the Arabian Sea. These cliffs are of tertiary sedimentary formation and are referred to by geologists as the Varkala Formation. They are recognized as a geological monument by the Geological Survey of India. Water spouts and spas are present along the sides of these cliffs.

Nearby Attractions and Activities

The Janardana Swami Temple is located near the beach and is known for its 10-day festival, Arattu, held twice a year.

Sivagiri Mutt, another notable attraction, is situated atop the Sivagiri hill. The Varkala Sivagiri Railway Station provides convenient access to the area.

Varkala Beach offers a variety of adventure and leisure activities such as boat riding, parasailing, jet skiing, and horse riding. Numerous restaurants and cafes line the beach, though alcohol is not served.
